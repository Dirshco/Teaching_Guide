Step 1: Developing Your Research Idea

üéØ Why This Matters for IMGs

As an IMG, research experience helps you:

  • Stand out in residency applications
  • Understand the U.S. healthcare system
  • Build relationships with U.S. mentors
  • Develop skills transferable to clinical practice

üìã Example: Our Telemedicine Study

Initial Observation: "During COVID, everyone used video visits. Now, I notice many patients prefer phone calls."

Research Question: "What are the current patterns of telemedicine utilization in office-based care, and do patients prefer audio or video visits?"

Why it works: Specific, measurable, relevant, and uses available data.

üß† Brainstorm Your Own Idea

Think about:

  1. What clinical patterns have you observed?
  2. What questions came up during your rotations?
  3. What healthcare topics interest you most?
What makes a good research question for database studies?
Specific, measurable, and answerable with available data
Broad enough to cover many topics at once
Based only on personal clinical experience
Requiring experimental intervention

Database Setup Guide

üîë MEPS Registration Walkthrough

  1. Go to meps.ahrq.gov
  2. Click "Data Center" ‚Üí "MEPS Data Files"
  3. Select year (2023 recommended for current data)
  4. Download "Office-Based Medical Provider Visits" file
  5. Download corresponding documentation (codebook)

üìä R Code for Telemedicine Analysis

# Load necessary packages
library(tidyverse)  # Data manipulation
library(survey)     # For survey weights
library(ggplot2)    # For graphs

# 1. Load MEPS data
meps_data <- read_csv("MEPS_2023_OfficeVisits.csv")

# 2. Calculate telemedicine percentage
tele_percent <- meps_data %>%
  summarize(tele_percent = mean(TelehealthFlag == "Yes", na.rm = TRUE) * 100)

# 3. Create modality distribution
modality_dist <- meps_data %>%
  filter(TelehealthFlag == "Yes") %>%
  count(Modality) %>%
  mutate(percent = n / sum(n) * 100)

# 4. Basic bar chart
ggplot(modality_dist, aes(x = Modality, y = percent, fill = Modality)) +
  geom_bar(stat = "identity") +
  theme_minimal() +
  labs(title = "Telemedicine Modality Distribution",
       y = "Percentage (%)")

üìà Step-by-Step Analysis Plan

  1. Descriptive Statistics: Means, percentages, counts
  2. Univariate Analysis: One variable at a time
  3. Bivariate Analysis: Compare two variables (t-test, chi-square)
  4. Multivariate Analysis: Regression if needed (optional for beginners)

Step 4: Writing Your First Manuscript

üìù IMRAD Structure Made Simple

I = Introduction

Length: 250-300 words

Answer: Why does this matter?

M = Methods

Length: 300-400 words

Answer: How did you study it?

R = Results

Length: 200-300 words

Answer: What did you find?

D = Discussion

Length: 400-500 words

Answer: What does it mean?

üî§ Abstract Template (Fill in the Blanks)

TITLE: [Brief, descriptive title about your topic]

BACKGROUND: [1-2 sentences about why this matters] Previous studies have shown [mention knowledge gap].

METHODS: We analyzed [database name and year] data. Our sample included [number] patients/visits. We examined [main variables].

RESULTS: We found [key percentage or finding]. Specifically, [second important finding].

CONCLUSIONS: [Main takeaway message]. These findings suggest [implication for practice or policy].
